Assignment 1 · Data Visualization Project

Assignment 1
Coursework · Data Visualization

This storyboard project turns one week of real calorie intake and food spending into a “fighting-game” style animation. Using BMR/TDEE estimates and a detailed log of meals, costs, and activities, I map health and money bars to daily energy balance and budget status, and narrate how small choices add up to a failed or successful weight-loss week. The goal is to make diet tracking more narrative, visual, and emotionally relatable instead of just tables and charts.

Assignment 2 · Presentation Slides

Assignment 2
Slides · Presentation

In this assignment, I reconstruct and extend John Snow’s 1854 cholera map using modern tools such as QGIS, OpenStreetMap/Overpass Turbo, Blender, and Maya. I first rebuild the classic death and pump map and heatmap to recover Snow’s core insight around the Broad Street pump, then layer in a contemporary population-density proxy and 3D urban massing to explore how outbreak risk relates to where people live and move. The final concept is an interactive timeline interface that lets viewers see cases, density, and key events unfold together in space and time.

Assignment 3 · Final Report

Assignment 3
Final Report · Data Story

This persuasive infographic visualizes the decline in Canada’s total fertility rate from 2004 to 2024 using a stepped pathway of platforms and a recurring child character to personify each year’s data point. It contrasts the replacement-level fertility benchmark (2.1) with actual values, highlights the temporary rebound and subsequent sharp drop around the COVID-19 period, and frames 2024’s 1.25 rate as a historically low level with long-term demographic implications. The design is built from Statistics Canada data and paired with a conventional line chart on the last page for analytical reference.
